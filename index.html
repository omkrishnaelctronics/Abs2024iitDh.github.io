About

I am an MS by Research scholar in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at IIT Dharwad, working at the intersection of deep learning, signal processing, and medical imaging. My work asks a simple question with a hard answer: how do we build models that respect the structure of the data — temporal, spatial, or multimodal — instead of brute-forcing their way through it?

That question has taken me from wavelet-guided architectures for time series, to lightweight segmentation networks for endoscopic and dermatological imaging, to vision–language frameworks for radiology report generation. Recently, I've been pulled toward large language models, agentic systems, and the broader question of how multimodal models reason, not just perceive.

"निश्चयं बुद्धिर् वीर्यं न भयम् हनुमत् समम् — विद्या विनययुक्तस्य जयः सदा सुनिश्चितः"

Before research, I spent time in industry — debugging BLE stacks at boAt Lifestyle and writing ROS control software at Cobottica Automation — which is probably why I still care, perhaps more than is fashionable in deep learning, about whether a model is actually deployable.

Projects

EFSVMNet — Enhanced Spatial Feature Selective Vision State-Space Model

Jan 2025 – May 2025

with Sachin Maurya

  • Built an enhanced VM-UNet variant with a lightweight Spatial Feature Selective (SFS) module to reduce feature redundancy and overfitting in medical image segmentation.
  • Proposed a feature importance mask and a novel spatial feature selective loss for adaptive suppression of uninformative regions.
  • Outperformed CNN, Transformer, and state-space baselines across six benchmarks — ISIC 2017/2018, Kvasir-SEG, CVC-ClinicDB, CVC-ColonDB, ETIS.
